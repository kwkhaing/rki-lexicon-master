## Tone

Rakhine is a tonal language. The following tones are distinguished:

| Tone | IPA | Description | Example |
|------|-----|-------------|---------|
| High | ◌́  | High level tone | /ká/ |
| Low | ◌̀  | Low level tone | /kà/ |
| Falling | ◌̂  | High falling tone | /kâ/ |
| Creaky | ◌̰  | Creaky voice tone | /kà̰/ |

## Syllable Structure

The basic syllable structure in Rakhine is:

(C)(G)V(C)(T)

Where:
- C = consonant
- G = glide (j, w)
- V = vowel
- T = tone

Examples:
- CV: /ka/ (က)
- CVC: /kan/ (ကန်)
- CGV: /kja/ (ကျ)
- CVT: /ká/ (က) with high tone

## Phonological Processes

### Tone Sandhi
Tone changes when words combine in certain contexts.

### Final Consonant Neutralization
Some final consonants are neutralized in pronunciation.

### Vowel Harmony
Limited vowel harmony exists in some dialects.

## Dialectal Variations

### Sittwe Dialect
- Clear tone distinctions
- Preservation of final stops

### Mrauk-U Dialect
- Vowel shifts
- Tone merging in some contexts

## Transcription Conventions

### Phonemic Transcription
Use slashes: /ká/

### Phonetic Transcription
Use brackets: [ká]

### Tone Marking
Place tone marks on the vowel: /mà/ vs /má/
